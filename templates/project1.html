Climate Change Awareness and Plantation Drive – The Citizens Foundation School, Kohrian

Our team visited The Citizens Foundation School, Kohrian as part of our Climate Change Awareness Initiative, a program dedicated to educating young minds about pressing environmental issues and encouraging sustainable, community-driven actions. During the session, we engaged with students to discuss the fundamentals of climate change; its causes, global and local impacts, and the importance of adopting eco-friendly practices in daily life. We emphasized how small lifestyle changes, such as conserving water, reducing waste, and planting trees, can collectively make a significant difference in combating climate challenges. The highlight of the visit was an organized plantation drive, where students enthusiastically participated in planting saplings within their school premises. Our team guided them through proper plantation techniques, explained the importance of native species, and demonstrated methods for effective post-plantation care to ensure long-term growth and sustainability. Beyond the practical activity, we aimed to inspire a sense of environmental stewardship among the students—helping them realize their role as future changemakers capable of protecting and preserving their environment. This collaborative effort not only promoted awareness but also fostered a spirit of responsibility, teamwork, and hope. The event served as a meaningful reminder that even the smallest, consistent actions can collectively strengthen ecological resilience and pave the way for a greener, healthier planet for generations to come.
